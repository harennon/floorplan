# Gaming room

*Aesthetic:* an immersive, screen-centric room — either a focused **battlestation** or a lean-back **console lounge**. This context is `[SOFT]`-heavy by nature and the catalog is thin here, so this reference is prose-first and `[GEO]`-light. Every rule is `[ROT]` guidance; only feasibility and `check_brief.satisfied` ever gate.

## Pick one sub-mode from the brief / user ask

### Battlestation (desk + chair + screen, close)

- Place a `desk` against the **screen wall** (the focal wall `[SOFT]`), and pull the seat (`chair` / `armchair`) up to the desk.
- Eye-to-screen viewing distance for a desk monitor is ~**0.5–0.75 m** — close, unlike a TV.
- **Catalog gap:** there is no `monitor` type, and `tv` (min_w 0.90 m) is too large to stand in for a desk monitor. **Approximate** the screen by the **desk depth plus a shallow strip on the desk**, and treat the monitor as implied. Do **not** place a `tv` as a desk monitor — a wall-scale TV at 0.5–0.75 m is wrong for a battlestation. (A `monitor` / `gaming-chair` catalog type is deferred — see the catalog-gap follow-ups.)
- Orient the seat's front at the screen wall.

### Console / couch (sofa + TV, lean-back)

- Identical to the living-room TV geometry: a `sofa` on a wall, a `tv` opposite at **1.0–2.5× its diagonal** (living-room rule C2). Reuse `references/living-room.md` C2 for the distance formula rather than duplicating it here.
- This is the one gaming sub-mode where a wall-scale `tv` is correct.

## Focal `[SOFT]`

The **screen wall** is the focal. Orient the seat / desk front at it. In battlestation mode this is a `[SOFT]` focal wall — there is no fireplace/window competing.

## Ambience `[SOFT]` / prose only

Bias / ambient lighting behind the screen, a cable-managed desk, and a clear zone around the chair all improve the feel. **No lighting concept exists in the model**, so this is **narrative guidance only — never a checkable rule**. (A lighting / ambience layer is deferred — see the catalog-gap follow-ups.)

## Circulation

Still keep the ≥ 0.915 m traffic path to the seat clear (the common-core walkway rule in SKILL.md). The close desk-to-chair or sofa-to-TV gaps are intra-zone, not walkway paths — don't spread them to hit the walkway number.

## Types in play

`desk`, `chair`, `armchair`, `sofa`, `tv`, `bookshelf` (for storage).

## Real sizes (read `floorplan://catalog`)

- `desk` 1.00–1.80 m wide, 0.60–0.80 m deep — the depth is what you use to approximate the monitor strip.
- `chair` 0.43–0.55 m; `armchair` 0.65–1.10 m.
- `sofa` / `tv` bounds as in the living-room reference (for the console sub-mode).
