Why a popover

Keeps the inspector chrome tiny — one material chip whose disc shows the current color. Tapping opens a grouped board of ~24 curated swatches. Scales to more materials without crowding the toolbar.

Curated, not a hex wheel

Swatches are grouped by real material families (woods, upholstery neutrals, appliance finishes, accents). Realistic defaults beat infinite choice — matches the "fun but real tool" philosophy.

Auto = theme ink

The first dashed cell resets to Auto (unset). Renders with today's theme fill and stays legible in both light & dark. Old plans / share links keep working with no color field.

Direction B · inline quick strip
most-common swatches inline; “+” opens the full board

Trade-off

Zero-click access to the 6 most-used materials, but eats horizontal space and pushes the rarer swatches behind a “+”. Better on desktop, awkward on the mobile bottom-sheet inspector.

Shared component

The floor picker is the same swatch board, opened from a room’s inspector. Floors render at low opacity so furniture stays legible on top; the board seeds the Floors group (woods, tile, concrete) first.

Category-aware defaults

New pieces can seed a sensible default per category — floors → oak, appliances → white — so a plan looks good with zero picking. Optional; Auto remains the true fallback.
