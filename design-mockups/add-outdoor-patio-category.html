3 · Why these glyphs read at a glance

Table vs. parasol are the design risk: both are round. They are distinguished by (a) footprint — table is 0.90 m and square-bounded, parasol is 2.00 m and dominates its box; and (b) the parasol adds diagonal rib lines (an open canopy), while the table keeps only orthogonal cross-lines (a solid top). If reviewers find them too similar at small sizes, an alternative is to draw the parasol canopy as a dashed circle (open/shade) vs. the table's solid circle — still primitive-only. Flag your preference and the LLD will lock it.

Chair reuses the sofa idiom (backrest strip across the top ~25% + inset seat) so the outdoor set feels of-a-piece with the living set. Planter is a small square box with a foliage circle — deliberately distinct from the round chair glyph in the living row.

4 · Type names & data (for the LLD)

Proposed SymbolType values: patio_table, patio_chair, parasol, planter. Each gets a CATALOG entry with category: "outdoor". These flow unchanged through create → place → resize → rotate → clearance → export (PNG/SVG/JSON) → URL-hash, and MCP place_symbol picks them up from CATALOG with no allowlist edit.
