Room-center / mid-line snap

The last piece of the alignment set (#23): a dragged symbol snaps to the room's centroid and to its horizontal / vertical mid-lines — "center the table in the room." Reuses the LLD-34 transient guide + chip renderer; guides live only during a drag and vanish on drop. Below: two guide-styling directions for the room-center cue, the three snap variants, where it slots in the precedence order, and a live playground.

Direction — how the room-center cue reads
Snap variants — what aligns to the room
Precedence — one resolution order in the snap path
  • 1Alt held → raw world point. Full transient bypass, independent of the toggle.
  • 2Wall-flush (#1) → seat the near edge against a wall face. Owns the perpendicular axis.
  • 3Object alignment (#2) → nearest in-range edge/center of another symbol, per-axis.
  • 4Room-center / mid-line (this issue) → per-axis snap of the symbol center to the room centroid or a room mid-line. Same tier as object alignment; whichever target is nearer on that axis wins. Beats the grid, loses to wall-flush.
  • 5Grid → nearest grid line on any axis not yet claimed.
  • 6Raw → the untouched pointer world point.

Room-center is gated by the same Snap toggle as object-alignment and grid (all read prefs.gridSnap()). It is a finer snap than object-alignment, so its threshold is the same tight ~8 px; a nearby wall or neighbouring symbol on a contested axis still wins.
