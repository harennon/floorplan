Object-to-object alignment snapping

Transient drag guides that snap a symbol's edges and centers to neighbouring symbols — the Figma / SketchUp pattern, in the blueprint idiom. Guides live only during a drag and vanish on drop. Below: three guide-styling directions, the snap types, the precedence order, and a live playground.

Snap types — what aligns
Precedence — one resolution order in the snap path
  • 1Alt held → raw world point. Full transient bypass, independent of any toggle.
  • 2Wall-flush (#1) → seat the near edge against a wall face. Owns the perpendicular axis.
  • 3Object alignment (this issue) → nearest in-range edge/center target on each axis, evaluated per-axis so it can co-exist with wall-flush on the orthogonal axis. Beats the grid.
  • 4Grid → snapStep() when the snap toggle is on and no closer alignment target exists.
  • 5Raw → toggle off, no target in range.

Per-axis resolution keeps wall-flush and alignment from fighting: e.g. a sofa can seat flush to a wall (Y) while its left edge aligns to a bookshelf (X) in the same drag.
