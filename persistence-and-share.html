Persistence & Share

The plan is always safe (localStorage autosave) and always sendable (share link · PNG · SVG · JSON). All chrome reuses the existing warm-blueprint language: floating panels, hairline gold borders, backdrop blur, DM Mono labels. Nothing hits a server — the share link encodes the whole plan in the URL #hash.

Two new pieces of floating chrome, plus banners/toasts. Everything else is existing app furniture, shown here for context. Click the buttons below — the share sheet, copy, export menu, and toasts are live.

1 · Top-right chrome (in situ)

The unit toggle already lives top-right. We add — from left — the save-status pill (quiet, ambient) and a Share button that opens the sheet, plus a small overflow ⋯ menu holding Export / Import / Reset. This keeps the primary artifact action ("Share") one tap away without crowding the canvas.

Saved
2 · Autosave status pill — states

Debounced autosave to localStorage (write ~500ms after the last edit). The pill is ambient, never blocking. It flips to Saving… during the write and settles to Saved. On a private-mode / quota failure it shows a non-alarming Not saved so a user knows to export manually.

3 · Share sheet — the hub

One modal collects every artifact. Top: the share link (plan → compressed → base64 in the #hash), one-tap copy, and a QR for phone hand-off. Below a divider: export tiles (PNG / SVG / JSON) and a quiet import affordance. This is the full sheet, rendered inline for review.

4 · Banners & toasts

When someone opens a share link, we parse the #hash and, if a local plan also exists, show a top-center restore banner so they choose rather than silently clobbering their own work. Routine confirmations (copied, exported, imported) use quiet auto-dismissing toasts.
