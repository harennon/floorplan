Draw & snap with a finger — without the point landing where you can't see it

Drawing walls on a phone has one hard problem: your fingertip covers the exact grid point you're trying to snap to. LLD 46 solved this by committing the vertex 56 px above the finger (an "offset loupe"). Field feedback: that snaps too high — the point lands away from where the finger is, so it feels wrong.

These three directions all keep the commit point directly under the finger (no offset) and solve occlusion a different way. Everything else in scope — two-finger pinch/pan disambiguation, finger-sized handles, page-gesture suppression, the bottom-sheet dock — is shared across all three and shown below. Tap a phone to place vertices.

Reverses the offset-loupe decision from LLD 46 per the issue comment "snaps too high, don't use offset." The vertex now commits under the fingertip.
Recommended · Option A

In-place magnifier loupe

The snap point stays exactly under the fingertip. A round magnifier bubble floats above the finger and shows a zoomed live view of that hidden point, with the snap crosshair centred. You aim by watching the bubble; the vertex commits where your finger actually is.

snapgrid seg0
  • Occlusion solved without moving the commit point — no "too high" feel.
  • Bubble only appears while a finger is down in draw mode; hidden for mouse/pen.
  • Reuses the existing snap glyph; the loupe is a render-only overlay.
  • Costs a second cheap redraw of the canvas region into the bubble.
Option B

Big glyph + tether (no magnifier)

Simplest change. Commit point stays under the finger; the snap glyph just grows larger under a coarse pointer (a ~28 px teal diamond instead of 10 px) and pokes out from under the fingertip, with a short tether so you can read which grid point it locked onto. No zoom, no extra render pass.

snapgrid seg0
  • Cheapest to build — a coarse-pointer glyph size bump, no new module.
  • The glyph edge peeks past the finger; fine points can still be partly hidden.
  • Best when paired with the higher default zoom on touch (bigger cells).
Option C

Tap-then-nudge

A coarse tap places the vertex under the finger (no offset), then a small thumb-reachable D-pad lets you nudge it one grid cell at a time to fine-tune before the next point. Precision without needing to see under the finger at all — good for one-handed use.

snapgrid seg0
  • Most precise and fully one-handed, but adds a step and on-screen chrome.
  • The D-pad occupies a corner of the canvas; extra UI to learn.
  • Nudge acts on the last-placed point until the next tap.

Select mode: touch-sized rotate/resize handles (left = touch, right = desktop mouse) and the swipeable bottom-sheet dock. Drag the selected chair; the handles are hittable with a thumb.

toolselect
  • Handle sizing is gated by matchMedia("(any-pointer:coarse)"), evaluated once at load — mouse path keeps 12/14 px targets.
  • Dock becomes a full-width bottom sheet on coarse/narrow screens, sitting above the OS home-indicator via env(safe-area-inset-bottom).
  • Dock swipe (touch-action:pan-x) pages symbols horizontally and never reaches the canvas — it owns its own pointer capture.
  • Gesture disambiguation: a _gestureCancelled flag blocks the trailing commit when a 2nd finger interrupts a draw or move.
